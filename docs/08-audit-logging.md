## What Audit Logging Means

Audit logging means recording important system actions so they can be reviewed later. These logs help show who performed an action, what action was taken, when it happened, and in which tenant or account context it occurred.

## Why It Matters in Mojakey

Mojakey supports child-related workflows, family accounts, institution operations, and payment-related events. Because these actions can affect safety, privacy, billing, and dispute handling, the platform needs reliable audit logs to support traceability, operational review, and incident investigation.

## Events That Should Be Logged

- Login and logout activity
- Email verification events
- Identity verification results
- Child check-in and check-out actions
- Enrollment changes
- Payment or billing-related changes
- Role or permission changes
- Administrative updates

## Risks if Logs Are Weak or Missing

If audit logs are weak or missing, the platform may not be able to explain who performed a sensitive action, when it happened, or whether data was changed improperly. This makes dispute handling, security investigations, and operational review much harder, especially in a system involving child attendance and payment-related workflows.
