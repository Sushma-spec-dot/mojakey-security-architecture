## What Incident Response Readiness Means

Incident response readiness means designing the platform so suspicious activity, security events, or operational disputes can be investigated effectively. The system should retain enough trustworthy context to understand what happened, who performed the action, when it happened, and which tenant or account was involved.

## Why It Matters in Mojakey

Mojakey supports child-related workflows, family accounts, institution operations, and payment-related events. If a dispute, suspicious login, unauthorized check-out, or data access concern occurs, the platform should make it possible to investigate quickly and accurately.

## Readiness Measures

- Maintain reliable audit logs for sensitive actions.
- Capture actor identity, timestamp, and tenant context for important events.
- Preserve attendance, enrollment, and billing-related history.
- Make role and permission changes traceable.
- Support review of QR-based and admin-triggered actions.

## Risks if Readiness Is Weak

If incident response readiness is weak, Mojakey may struggle to investigate disputes, explain suspicious activity, or confirm whether a sensitive action was legitimate. This can delay resolution, reduce trust, and make operational or security incidents harder to contain.
