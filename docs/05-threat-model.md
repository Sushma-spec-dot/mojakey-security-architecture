# What are we protecting


parent and guardian account data

child profile data

attendance and check-in/check-out records

enrollment and payment records

institution operational data

QR-based verification tokens

audit logs

role and permission assignments

#  Who are we protecting from


unauthorized external users

malicious insiders

users accessing the wrong tenant data

attackers replaying or forging QR actions

people trying to alter attendance or billing records

## Example Threats

- An unauthorized user tries to access a parent or child account.
- A staff member attempts to view or modify data from another institution.
- A forged or replayed QR code is used to perform an invalid check-in or check-out.
- An attacker tries to tamper with attendance, enrollment, or billing-related records.
- A privileged user makes sensitive changes without proper logging or review.

## Why the Threats Matter

These threats matter because Mojakey handles child-related workflows, family data, institution operations, and payment-related events. A security failure could lead to privacy violations, unauthorized child pickup actions, incorrect billing, cross-tenant data exposure, or the inability to investigate disputes and incidents properly.
